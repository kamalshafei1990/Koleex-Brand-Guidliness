19.3 — Design Principles

These 16 rules govern the visual design of all stationery and corporate materials. They ensure that every piece maintains the Koleex brand identity with absolute consistency.

Minimal layout with maximum clarity
Clean typography using only approved fonts
Large white space to create breathing room
Structured grid for all content alignment
Balanced alignment across all elements
Limited color palette — black, white, gray, silver
Clear visual hierarchy from title to footer
Consistent margins on all document types
Thin dividers to separate content sections
No decorative graphics of any kind
No icons in stationery design (logo only)
No colorful backgrounds on any material
No gradients on any printed surface
Black, white, and gray tones only
Visual consistency across all 21 items
Premium feel through restraint and precision
19.4 — Layout Structure

Every stationery and corporate document must follow a consistent layout structure. The placement of the logo, document title, content area, and footer information must be predictable and professional across all materials.

Logo placed top-left or top-center
Document title clearly visible
Structured content area
Consistent margins on all sides
Footer with company information
Page numbers on multi-page documents
Thin dividers between sections
Proper spacing between all elements
Clear hierarchy from header to footer
19.5 — Typography in Stationery

Typography in stationery must follow a strict hierarchy. Each level has a defined purpose, and no deviation is permitted. Font sizes must be appropriate for the material size, and decorative or display fonts are strictly prohibited.

Use only approved brand fonts (Inter for digital, approved serif/sans for print)
Maintain consistent font sizes proportional to the document size
Use proper line spacing (1.4–1.6 for body text)
Ensure adequate contrast between text and background
Never use decorative, script, or display fonts on any stationery
Never stretch, compress, or distort any typeface
Never use more than 2 font weights on a single piece of stationery
Never use letter-spacing that reduces legibility
19.6 — Printing & Material Guidelines

The choice of paper, finish, and printing technique directly impacts the perceived quality of the brand. KOLEEX materials must always feel premium to the touch. Only approved materials and finishes are permitted.

Recommended Materials
  • Matte paper — premium uncoated finish
  • Textured paper — subtle linen or cotton feel
  • Thick card stock — 350gsm+ for business cards
  • Smooth white paper — bright white, high opacity
  • Black matte premium — for premium card variants
  • Emboss / deboss finishing — tactile brand mark
  • Foil stamping — silver or black foil on select items
Materials to Avoid
  • Glossy paper — creates cheap, reflective appearance
  • Cheap thin paper — below 120gsm for any document
  • Bright white glossy card — feels plasticky
  • Low quality printing — inkjet or low-res output
  • Incorrect color reproduction — off-brand tones

19.8 — Visual Presentation

All stationery and corporate materials must be presented using realistic mockups, not flat layouts. Mockups must communicate premium quality, modern aesthetics, and professional craftsmanship. The visual style must match the brand identity at every level.

Mockup Requirement

All brand materials must be presented in realistic 3D mockups with proper perspective, shadows, and studio-quality lighting. Flat 2D layouts are not acceptable for any external or client-facing presentation.
