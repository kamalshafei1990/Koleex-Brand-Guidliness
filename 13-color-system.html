Color System

The official color system defining how colors are used, controlled, and applied to maintain a minimal, professional, and technology-oriented brand identity.

COLOR IDENTITY SYSTEM

Color in the Koleex brand is not decorative. It is structural.

The Koleex color system is built on the principle of maximum clarity with minimum visual noise. Every color in the palette has a defined purpose, a controlled application, and a strict hierarchy. The brand relies on black and white as its primary visual language, supported by a carefully curated set of neutral grays. Accent colors are permitted only in highly controlled, functional contexts. This system ensures that every visual output from Koleex communicates precision, professionalism, and industrial authority.

Minimal Palette High Contrast Controlled Usage
Primary Color Philosophy

The Koleex brand is built on two foundational colors: Black and White. These are not just colors — they are the visual language of the brand. Together, they create the maximum possible contrast, the clearest possible communication, and the most timeless visual identity.

Black represents industrial strength, engineering precision, and corporate authority
White represents clarity, breathing space, and modern minimalism
Together they create the highest possible contrast ratio for legibility
The combination is timeless — it will never feel dated or trendy
Black and white work across all media: digital, print, signage, packaging
They reduce visual noise and force content to speak for itself

Core Design Principle

If a design does not look good in black and white, then the design system is not correct. Color should enhance, not rescue. Every Koleex design must first be evaluated in pure black and white before any additional color is considered.

Neutral Colors

Neutral gray tones serve as the structural backbone of the Koleex visual system. They create depth, hierarchy, and separation between content layers without introducing visual distraction.

Section backgrounds and content separation
Secondary text, subtitles, and captions
Borders, dividers, and structural lines
Table backgrounds, card surfaces, and form fields
Disabled states, placeholder text, and inactive UI elements
Data visualization supporting elements and chart grids
Neutral colors must always maintain readable contrast against text. Never use gray text on a gray background without sufficient contrast ratio (minimum 4.5:1 for body text).
Accent Colors

Accent colors are used sparingly and only in functional, interactive contexts. The primary accent color is a controlled blue (#0071E3) used exclusively for interactive UI elements, hyperlinks, and call-to-action buttons. Accent colors must never dominate a layout.

Accent Color Rule

Accent colors must never exceed 8% of the total visible area of any design. They exist to guide the user's eye, not to decorate. If an accent color is removed and the design loses meaning, the design is too dependent on color and must be restructured.

Gradient Usage

Gradients are a special visual tool in the Koleex system. They are not part of everyday usage and must be applied only in premium, high-impact contexts with strict rules.

Gradients allowed in:
  • Hero sections of websites and landing pages
  • Premium product visuals and feature highlights
  • Motion graphics and video intros
  • Exhibition and event backdrop designs
  • Special edition packaging accents
Gradients should not be used in:
  • Logo or logo backgrounds
  • Body text or paragraph backgrounds
  • Official documents, invoices, or contracts
  • Business cards or letterheads
  • Standard UI components or navigation

Gradient Rules:

Gradients must only use colors from the approved palette (black, white, gray tones)
Gradients must be subtle and never oversaturated or neon-like
Gradients must maintain legibility of any overlaid text or logo
Gradients should transition smoothly without visible banding
No more than two gradient colors per application
Rainbow or multi-color gradients are strictly prohibited
Color in Different Applications

Each brand touchpoint has specific color rules to ensure consistency. The following guidelines define how color is applied across key application types.

Posters
  • Black or white dominant backgrounds
  • White or black headline text only
  • Gray for subtitles and secondary info
  • Accent color for one CTA element maximum
  • No colorful imagery as background
Website
  • White page background as default
  • Black navigation bar and footer
  • Gray sections for content separation
  • Accent blue for links and buttons only
  • Dark mode: invert black/white, keep grays
UI / Software
  • White canvas, black text hierarchy
  • Gray for sidebar, borders, and disabled states
  • Accent for primary actions and active states
  • Error red and success green for system states
  • No decorative color in functional interfaces
Packaging
  • White or matte black box surfaces
  • Black logo and product name
  • Gray for specifications and technical text
  • No color images or illustrations on package
  • Minimal, clean, editorial layout
Documents
  • White paper, black body text
  • Black headings, gray subheadings
  • Gray for table borders and dividers
  • No colored backgrounds or highlighted text
  • Logo always in black on document headers
Exhibitions
  • Black booth structure, white panels
  • Large white logo on black surfaces
  • Controlled lighting (white or warm neutral)
  • Gray for informational signage
  • Accent only for digital screens or interactive elements

Color Do

Always follow these color guidelines across all Koleex brand materials:

Always use black and white as the primary color pair in every design
Use only the approved gray values from the official palette
Follow the 70/20/8/2 color distribution hierarchy
Ensure all text has a minimum contrast ratio of 4.5:1 against its background
Use accent colors only for interactive and functional elements
Test every design in black and white first before adding any color
Use gradients only in approved premium contexts with approved colors
Maintain consistency across all platforms — digital, print, and physical
Use the exact HEX values specified in this document — do not approximate
Keep packaging and documents strictly black, white, and gray
Color Don't

Never do any of the following in Koleex brand materials:

Do not use bright, saturated, or neon colors anywhere in the brand
Do not use rainbow gradients or multi-color backgrounds
Do not apply color to the logo — it must remain black, white, or gray
Do not use colored text on colored backgrounds (e.g. red text on blue)
Do not let accent colors exceed 8% of the visible design area
Do not use glow, neon, or shadow color effects on any element
Do not use unapproved gray values — stick to the defined palette
Do not use color as the only differentiator — structure must work without it
Do not use gradients in official documents, business cards, or forms
Do not introduce new colors without written approval from the brand team
Do not use color to make a design look "exciting" — the content must speak for itself
Do not use decorative color patterns, textures, or color overlays
