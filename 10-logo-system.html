Logo System

The complete logo identity system defining how the Koleex logo must appear, be protected, and applied across all brand touchpoints.

LOGO IDENTITY SYSTEM

The Koleex logo is the most important element of the entire brand identity.

The logo is the first visual impression of the brand. It must always be presented with precision, consistency, and respect. The Koleex wordmark was designed to reflect the industrial, technological, and modern identity of the group. Every detail — from the geometry of each letter to the spacing between them — was engineered to communicate strength, logic, and evolution.

The logo should feel
Powerful and confident Clean and engineered Modern and timeless Sharp and minimal Industrial and premium
The logo must never feel
Decorative or playful Casual or informal Trendy or temporary Soft or rounded Cheap or generic
Logo Role

The Koleex logo is the primary identifier of the brand across all media and touchpoints. It represents:

The corporate identity of Koleex International Group
The industrial and technological direction of the brand
The professionalism, precision, and quality of the company
The modern, engineered, and forward-thinking vision
Trust, authority, and long-term commitment
A global brand with roots in knowledge and execution

Logo Color Usage

The logo must only appear in the approved brand colors. Never apply unauthorized colors or effects to the logo.

Approved Colors
  • Black (#000000) on white or light backgrounds
  • White (#FFFFFF) on black or very dark backgrounds
  • Silver/Gray (#86868B) for subtle or secondary use
  • Single solid color only — no multicolor
Not Allowed
  • No gradient fill on the logo
  • No bright or neon colors
  • No multicolor or rainbow effects
  • No transparency or opacity below 100%
  • No metallic or 3D effects
  • No pattern fill or texture fill
Default rule: Always use Black logo on White background. Use reversed or gray only when the context requires it.
Logo Background Rules

The logo must always appear on approved backgrounds to maintain clarity and contrast.

Correct Backgrounds
  • Solid white (#FFFFFF)
  • Solid black (#000000)
  • Light gray (#F5F5F7 or lighter)
  • Clean dark surface with sufficient contrast
  • Photo background with dark overlay (80%+ opacity)
Incorrect Backgrounds
  • Busy or patterned backgrounds without overlay
  • Low contrast (gray logo on light gray)
  • Gradient backgrounds
  • Colorful or bright surfaces
  • Noisy photographs without overlay
  • Textured or woodgrain surfaces

Logo Clear Space

The clear space ensures the logo remains visually prominent and free from clutter. These rules must always be followed:

Minimum clear space equals 1x the height of the "K" letter on all sides
No text, icons, borders, or graphics may enter the clear space zone
The clear space applies equally to all four sides
When in doubt, add more space rather than less
The clear space rule applies to all logo variants
On business cards and small surfaces, maintain at least 0.5x K height

Logo Alignment

The logo should always be placed in a consistent position across all materials to build recognition.

Top-left is the default position for documents, websites, and headers
Center is used for covers, splash screens, and hero sections
Top-center is used for packaging, labels, and product boxes
Never place the logo at the bottom of a page or near the edge
Always maintain proper clear space around the logo
Align the logo to the grid system used in the layout

Logo on Packaging

Packaging placement should prioritize visibility and brand recognition.

Place logo on the front face of the package, top-center
Use black logo on white/light packaging
Use white logo on dark packaging
Maintain minimum clear space even on small packages
Logo must be the largest brand element on the package
Never wrap the logo around corners or curves
Logo on Products

When the logo appears on physical products, machines, or equipment, these rules apply:

Correct
  • Engraved or embossed logo on metal surfaces
  • Printed logo on clean flat surfaces
  • Laser-etched logo on product bodies
  • Logo plate or badge on equipment
  • Consistent sizing across product lines
Incorrect
  • Logo on curved or uneven surfaces without adaptation
  • Logo placed on moving parts
  • Logo that can be easily scratched or worn
  • Logo placed too small on large machinery
  • Logo in colors not matching the product finish

Logo Do

Always follow these guidelines when using the Koleex logo:

Always use the original, unmodified logo files
Maintain the clear space zone around the logo at all times
Use only approved logo colors: black, white, or gray
Use SVG or high-resolution PNG for digital applications
Use vector formats (AI, EPS, PDF) for print applications
Place the logo on clean, uncluttered backgrounds
Ensure high contrast between logo and background
Scale the logo proportionally at all times
Respect the minimum size requirements
Align the logo consistently across all brand materials
Logo Don't

Never do any of the following to the Koleex logo:

Do not stretch, squeeze, or distort the logo in any way
Do not rotate the logo at any angle
Do not apply gradient, shadow, glow, or 3D effects
Do not use unapproved colors on the logo
Do not outline the logo or use it as a stroke-only element
Do not place the logo on busy or low-contrast backgrounds
Do not add extra text, symbols, or decorations to the logo
Do not crop or mask any part of the logo
Do not use low-resolution or pixelated logo files
Do not recreate or redraw the logo manually
Do not use the logo smaller than the minimum allowed size
Do not animate the logo without approval from the brand team
Logo Consistency Rule

The logo is the single most important visual asset of the Koleex brand. Its consistent and correct usage is non-negotiable.

Mandatory Brand Compliance

Any use of the Koleex logo that deviates from the rules outlined in this document must be reviewed and approved by the brand management team before publication or production. Unauthorized modifications to the logo are strictly prohibited and may result in brand inconsistency and legal consequences. When in doubt, always refer to this guide or contact the brand team directly.
