Photography Style

The complete photography guidelines for Koleex. Every photograph must communicate professionalism, clarity, and industrial precision. No artistic filters, no dramatic effects — only clean, realistic, high-quality imagery.

Photography System

Professional Photography for an Industrial Brand

Koleex photography is not artistic expression — it is brand communication. Every image must be clean, well-lit, professionally composed, and true to reality. Photography across all categories follows strict rules to ensure consistency and quality.

Product Factory Office Events People Composition Color Reference
General Photography Principles

The 28 fundamental principles that govern all Koleex photography. These rules apply to every photograph used in any brand material, regardless of category or context. Positive rules define what to achieve; negative rules define what to avoid.

What Every Photo Should Have

Clean environment — no dirt, dust, or debris visible
Organized background — intentional, tidy, and structured
Neutral colors — whites, grays, blacks, natural tones
Good lighting — even, bright, well-distributed illumination
Sharp focus — every important detail must be crisp
Straight camera angles — aligned with architectural lines
Level horizon — perfectly horizontal horizon line
Minimal clutter — only essential items in the frame
Simple composition — clear visual hierarchy
Clear subject — viewer immediately knows the focus
Professional quality — shot by a skilled photographer
High resolution — minimum 300 DPI for print, 72 DPI for web
Consistent color tone — matches Koleex neutral palette
No heavy filters — true-to-life color representation

What Every Photo Must Avoid

No strong color grading — no cinematic LUTs or presets
No dramatic shadows — avoid harsh, moody lighting
No artificial effects — no lens flares, bokeh overlays
No lens distortion — correct barrel/pincushion distortion
No tilted camera angles — no Dutch angles or creative tilts
No messy backgrounds — no cluttered or distracting scenes
No random objects — everything in frame must be intentional
No personal items in background — no coffee cups, phones, bags
No dirty environment — surfaces must be spotless
No low light noisy photos — avoid high ISO grain
No blurry photos — motion blur or out-of-focus is rejected
No overexposed photos — no blown-out highlights
No oversaturated photos — colors must remain natural
No heavy vignetting — edges must be evenly lit
Product Photography

Product photos are the most critical visual assets for Koleex. Every product must be shot on a clean white or black background, centered in the frame, with consistent lighting and minimal shadow. The product is the hero — nothing else competes for attention.

White or black background only — no gradients, no textures
Product centered in frame with generous white space
Soft, even lighting from multiple angles
Very subtle shadow below the product for grounding
Straight-on camera angle, level with the product
Consistent product angles across all catalog items
Minimum 3 angles per product: front, side, detail
No props, hands, or accessories unless required

Office Photography

Office photography represents the Koleex corporate environment. Photos should communicate modernity, organization, and professionalism. The office space must appear clean, well-designed, and aligned with the brand identity.

Clean, modern office spaces with minimal decoration
Neutral color palette — whites, grays, black furniture
Natural lighting preferred, supplemented with soft artificial light
Organized desks — no personal clutter or messy cables
Show meeting rooms, workstations, and collaborative spaces
Technology and screens should display Koleex branding when possible
Straight architectural lines — no lens distortion
Remove personal items, food, drinks from desks before shooting
Wide-angle shots for spaces, medium shots for work areas
Consistent color temperature across all office photos

Corporate portrait style — clean background, professional attire

Image Composition Rules

Composition rules ensure every Koleex photograph has visual clarity, balance, and intentional framing. These 14 rules must be followed by every photographer and designer working with Koleex imagery.

Center the main subject in the frame for product and portrait shots
Keep the horizon line perfectly level in all landscape shots
Maintain visual balance — equal weight on both sides of the frame
Crop intentionally — never cut off important elements awkwardly
Leave adequate breathing room around the subject (negative space)
Use vertical framing for tall subjects, horizontal for wide scenes
Separate foreground, midground, and background for depth
Guide the viewer's eye to the main subject using leading lines
Apply the rule of thirds for environmental and architectural shots
Eliminate all distracting elements from the edges of the frame
Maintain consistent aspect ratios across photo series
Use depth of field to isolate subjects when appropriate
Align architectural lines with frame edges — no converging verticals
Maintain consistent spacing and padding in multi-image layouts
Image Color Style

The color style of all Koleex photography must be neutral, realistic, and clean. No Instagram filters, no cinematic color grading, no warm or cool color casts. Colors should represent reality as accurately as possible.

Neutral white balance — no warm or cool color casts
Realistic colors — what you see in real life
Consistent color temperature across photo series
Slight desaturation is acceptable for a clean, professional look

Color Grading is Prohibited

Never apply LUTs, presets, or color grading to Koleex photographs. Only basic adjustments (exposure, contrast, white balance) are permitted during post-processing. The goal is to enhance reality, not create a mood.

Photography Do

These are the 22 rules that must be followed in every Koleex photograph. These rules ensure consistency, quality, and brand alignment across all visual materials.

Use clean, white, or neutral backgrounds for all product shots
Ensure even, professional lighting in every photograph
Keep the camera level and straight — no tilted angles
Maintain sharp focus on the primary subject at all times
Shoot at the highest resolution available on the camera
Use a tripod for product, office, and architectural photography
Clean the environment thoroughly before any photo session
Remove all personal items and clutter from the background
Correct white balance in post-production for natural colors
Maintain consistent color tone across all images in a series
Use the Koleex neutral palette as a reference for color grading
Capture multiple angles of every subject for selection flexibility
Ensure Koleex branding is visible in all event and exhibition photos
Use professional corporate attire in all people photography
Apply minimal post-processing — exposure, contrast, sharpness only
Organize and label all photo files according to the naming convention
Get brand team approval before publishing any photograph
Deliver photos in both RAW and high-resolution JPEG formats
Use natural light when possible, supplemented by studio lights
Frame architectural shots with corrected perspective distortion
Keep composition simple — one clear subject per photograph
Test all images on screen and print before final approval
Photography Don't

These are the 23 mistakes that must never appear in any Koleex photograph. Any image violating these rules will be rejected during the brand review process.

Don't use Instagram-style filters or presets on brand photography
Don't apply heavy color grading, LUTs, or cinematic looks
Don't use dramatic shadows, hard light, or moody lighting
Don't shoot with tilted or Dutch camera angles
Don't include messy, cluttered, or dirty backgrounds
Don't leave personal items (cups, phones, bags) visible in frame
Don't use artificial lens flares, bokeh overlays, or light leaks
Don't submit blurry, out-of-focus, or motion-blurred photographs
Don't submit overexposed photos with blown-out highlights
Don't submit underexposed, dark, or noisy low-light photos
Don't oversaturate colors — keep them natural and realistic
Don't use heavy vignetting or darkened edges
Don't use wide-angle lenses that create barrel distortion
Don't crop photos in ways that cut off important elements
Don't use stock photography as a substitute for original brand photos
Don't mix different photography styles within the same project
Don't add text overlays, watermarks, or graphics directly on photos
Don't use smartphone photos for professional brand materials
Don't publish any photo without brand team review and approval
Don't use HDR or tone-mapping effects that look unnatural
Don't use excessive skin retouching or face-altering filters
Don't shoot in environments with competing brand logos or signage
Don't use AI-generated images as substitutes for real photography
