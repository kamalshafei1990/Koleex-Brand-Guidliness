27 — MOTION & ANIMATION STYLE

Motion & Animation Style

Motion defines how the Koleex brand behaves across all digital touchpoints. Every animation must communicate technology, precision, and engineering excellence.

Motion System

Motion is the voice of precision in digital space

Motion defines brand behavior in digital environments including videos, website transitions, UI interactions, social media content, presentations, and exhibition screens. Every animation must communicate technology, precision, engineering, industrial systems, innovation, modernity, and professionalism. Motion must never feel playful, cartoonish, or decorative.

Videos Website Transitions UI Interactions Social Media Presentations Exhibition Screens
27.1 Purpose

Motion defines brand behavior in digital environments. It applies across videos, website transitions, UI interactions, social media, presentations, and exhibition screens. Every animation must reinforce the Koleex identity of technology, precision, and engineering excellence.

Motion Must Communicate

Technology — Advanced, digital-first, systematic approach
Precision — Exact, calculated, intentional movement
Engineering — Mechanical, structural, purposeful
Industrial Systems — Controlled, systematic, grid-based
Innovation — Forward-thinking, modern techniques
Professional — Clean, refined, corporate-grade

Never Playful or Decorative

Motion must never feel playful, cartoonish, or decorative. Avoid bouncing, elastic, spinning, or flashing animations. Every movement must have purpose and reinforce the industrial precision of the Koleex brand.

27.2 Motion Style Principles

All Koleex motion follows a set of core principles that ensure consistency across every digital touchpoint. Smooth, controlled, and grid-aligned movement defines the brand in motion.

Smooth & Controlled

Fluid movement with controlled speed, never abrupt or jerky

Minimal Movement

Only essential movement, nothing decorative or excessive

Grid-Based

All motion follows the grid system, structured and aligned

Clean Transitions

Fade, slide, and mask reveals only, no complex effects

Typography Animation

Text animates with purpose using fade, slide, and reveal

Line & Grid Animation

Structural elements animate along grid lines

Interface-Style

Motion inspired by premium UI/UX interfaces

Industrial Mechanical

Inspired by mechanical and engineering movement

Consistent Timing

Unified timing curves and durations across all platforms

Avoid These Motion Styles

Bouncing / Elastic
Cartoon / Playful
Spinning / Rotating
Flashing / Blinking
Overly Fast
Decorative Particles

27.5 UI Animation

UI animations must be fast but smooth, typically 150–300ms. Use fade combined with slide, no bounce or elastic effects. Minimal movement that guides the user, never distracts. The feel should be comparable to Apple, Tesla, or BMW interfaces.

27.8 Motion Do & Don't

Follow these rules strictly to maintain brand consistency across all motion and animation outputs.

Do
  • Use smooth, controlled, precise motion
  • Keep animations minimal and purposeful
  • Follow the grid system in all motion
  • Use fade, slide, and mask reveal transitions
  • Keep UI animations between 150–300ms
  • Use consistent easing curves (cubic-bezier(.4,0,.2,1))
  • Animate typography with fade and slide only
  • Keep logo animation duration 2–4 seconds
  • Use slow camera movement in video content
  • Maintain dark/neutral backgrounds in motion graphics
  • Use high contrast lighting in video
  • Stagger card and element appear animations
  • Use industrial and mechanical motion inspiration
  • Test motion on multiple devices and screen sizes
Don't
  • Use bouncing or elastic easing
  • Apply cartoon or playful animation styles
  • Use spinning or rotating transitions
  • Add flashing or blinking effects
  • Make animations overly fast or jarring
  • Use decorative particles or confetti
  • Apply 3D flip or cube transitions
  • Use explosion or shatter effects
  • Add random or chaotic motion paths
  • Use meme-style or funny video transitions
  • Apply too many simultaneous animations
  • Use bright neon color animations
  • Make text rotate or bounce on entry
  • Ignore timing consistency across platforms
