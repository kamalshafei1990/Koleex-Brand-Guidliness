24.4 Typography

Use Helvetica Neue as the primary typeface for all print materials. Bold weight for titles, medium and regular for descriptions. Maintain consistent hierarchy and avoid decorative fonts.

Typography Hierarchy

Title Industrial Precision
Subtitle Advanced CNC Technology Solutions
Body High-performance industrial equipment designed for modern manufacturing environments.
Footer www.koleex.com | Precision. Performance. Technology.
Bold weight for titles and headlines
Medium/regular weight for descriptions
Consistent hierarchy across all materials
Avoid decorative or script fonts
24.5 Color Usage

Black background is preferred for print materials. White text for primary content, gray for secondary information. Accent colors are used for highlights only. Brand gradients are applicable when following brand direction.

Avoid

Do NOT use bright random colors, neon colors, too many colors in one material, or cartoon-style color palettes. Keep it minimal, dark, and professional. Brand gradients are only applicable when following official brand direction.

24.6 Photography

Photography for print materials should convey industrial precision, technology, and quality. Use professional product photography, industrial machines, factory environments, technology details, and metal textures.

Product photography
Industrial machines
Factory environment
Technology details
Metal textures

Avoid

Stock photos, cartoon images, low-quality or pixelated images, generic lifestyle imagery, over-edited photos, or busy backgrounds.

24.10 Do & Don't

Summary of all print and marketing material design rules to ensure consistency and quality across all formats and material types.

Do
  • Use dark background (black or near-black)
  • Use white text for primary content
  • Use gray for secondary information
  • Use accent colors for highlights only
  • Maintain consistent margins and grid layout
  • Use Helvetica Neue for all text
  • Use bold for titles, regular for body
  • Use professional product photography
  • Keep layouts clean and minimal
  • Place logo consistently on every material
  • Use large margins and breathing room
  • Maintain clear typography hierarchy
  • Use tables for technical specifications
  • Use icons for features and benefits
  • Ensure readability from intended distance
Don't
  • Use bright or colorful backgrounds
  • Mix multiple fonts in one material
  • Create cluttered or busy layouts
  • Use neon or cartoon colors
  • Use low-quality or pixelated images
  • Use generic stock photography
  • Use cartoon or illustrated graphics
  • Stretch, rotate, or add effects to logo
  • Ignore consistent spacing and margins
  • Use decorative or script fonts
  • Use too many colors in one material
  • Over-edit or over-process images
  • Place text over busy imagery
  • Use random layout without grid system
  • Ignore brand color palette
