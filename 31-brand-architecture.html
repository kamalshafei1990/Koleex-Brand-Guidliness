Brand Architecture

How KOLEEX organizes its brands, sub-brands, product lines, systems, services, and platforms into a clear, scalable structure.

31.1 — What Is Brand Architecture?

Brand Architecture defines how brands, sub-brands, product lines, systems, and services are organized under a single company. It creates a clear structure that guides how products relate to each other and to the parent brand.

Organizes all brands, products, and services under one clear framework
Ensures clarity for customers, partners, and internal teams
Avoids brand confusion and naming conflicts
Enables smooth expansion into new markets and product lines
Provides consistent naming conventions across all offerings
Strengthens the connection between products and the master brand

A strong brand architecture gives every product, service, and platform a clear place within the KOLEEX ecosystem.

Without structure, brands become fragmented. With architecture, they become a system.

31.2 — KOLEEX Brand Structure

KOLEEX is the master brand. All product series, systems, software, platforms, services, sub-brands, and business divisions fall under KOLEEX. The KOLEEX name must always come first in any naming combination.

Critical Naming Rule

The KOLEEX name must always come FIRST. Write “KOLEEX NEXO” — never “NEXO by KOLEEX”. This applies to every product, system, service, and platform.

31.3 — Brand Architecture Model

KOLEEX follows the Branded House Model. One main brand, all products use the same name, with sub-names for individual lines, systems, software, and platforms.

KOLEEX Model

One master brand name powers all products and services. Sub-names are descriptors under the main brand.

  • Unified brand identity
  • Stronger brand recognition
  • Consistent customer experience
  • Easier expansion into new markets
  • Lower marketing costs per product

  • Higher marketing costs

31.8 — Do & Don't

Follow these rules to maintain brand architecture integrity across all products, platforms, and communications.

Do
  • Always place KOLEEX as the first word in any product name
  • Use short, strong, memorable sub-names
  • Follow the Branded House model for all new products
  • Maintain consistent naming across all languages
  • Use uppercase for KOLEEX and sub-names in official materials
  • Keep naming technology-focused and industrial in feeling
  • Align every new product name with the architecture hierarchy
  • Get brand team approval before creating new product names
  • Use the same naming structure for software and platforms
  • Document every new name in the brand architecture registry
Don't
  • Never place the product name before KOLEEX
  • Never create standalone brand names without KOLEEX
  • Never use abbreviations like KX or KLX in official names
  • Never mix uppercase and lowercase in brand names
  • Never add decorative words like “The” or “by” in names
  • Never use hyphens or special characters in product names
  • Never create names that sound consumer or lifestyle oriented
  • Never skip the brand team review for naming decisions
  • Never translate KOLEEX into other languages
  • Never use names longer than 3 words (KOLEEX + Name + Model)
